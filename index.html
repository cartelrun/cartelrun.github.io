CARTEL RUN | Synopsis

Failed singer, ROGELIO (60), is trying to commit suicide in his Mexican desert shack. He has to make it look like a murder so his estranged son can collect the life insurance. Rogelio has been waiting for the cartel to murder him, but they never showed up….

CARTEL RUN | Synopsis - cont'd

When American CAM (30) delivers his first shipment of AR-15s to the cartel, they send him with a gunman to execute Rogelio. They interrupt another suicide attempt and end up drunk, partying the night away THE HANGOVER style….

CARTEL RUN | Synopsis - cont'd

Next morning they rise realizing that Cam posted a video of them insulting both rival cartels, who have dispatched caravans of hitmen. Worse for Rogelio, his insurance plan ended at midnight….

CARTEL RUN | Synopsis - cont'd

With the border and airports out of the question, Cam’s only chance out of the country is to catch a boat to Cuba. Rogelio demands Cam’s 50K gun money to deliver him to Cancun. Not only are both cartels on their heels, but also Mexico’s deadliest assassin, LA SOMBRA….

CARTEL RUN / AUDIENCE

Audience

This action comedy celebrates the historic ties between the U.S. and Mexico, while giving an action audience what they want... guns, fast cars, and fighting.

CARTEL RUN / AUDIENCE

Though comic moments like having to drink urine to survive in the desert will floor the audience, heavier themes of suicide and abandonment will also give them a reason to care. Most American movies depict Mexico as one thing, either a desert or a beach.

CARTEL RUN / AUDIENCE

CARTEL RUN shows off Mexico in a hot-pursuit road movie with beautiful deserts, forrests, beaches, jungles, and Mexico City’s modern financial district. Though U.S. media and government depicts Mexico as a crime state, our story shows citizens who help our heroes along the way, out of pure kindness. Our heroes are changed by this journey, while they fight their generational gap of Baby Boomer vs. Millennial.
